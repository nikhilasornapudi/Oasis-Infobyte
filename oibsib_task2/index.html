Micheal Jackson

The King of Pop

Here's a timeline of Micheal's era:

  • 1958
    • Born in Stockholm on 29 August 1958. He was the eighth of ten children in the Jackson family.
  • 1964
    • Michael and Marlon joined the Jackson Brothers—a band formed by their father which included Jackie, Tito, and Jermaine—as backup musicians playing congas and tambourine.
  • 1965
    • Michael began sharing lead vocals with Jermaine, and the group's name was changed to the Jackson 5.
  • 1968
    • Motown signs The Jackson 5.
  • 1970
    • "I Want You Back" became the first Jackson 5 song to reach number one on the US Billboard Hot 100; it stayed there for four weeks.
    • Three more singles with Motown topped the chart: "ABC", "The Love You Save", and "I'll Be There".
  • 1975
    • The Jackson 5 left Motown, signing with Epic Records and renaming themselves the Jacksons.
  • 1978
    • Jackson makes his film debut as the Scarecrow in The Wiz, an urban retelling of the classic film The Wizard Of Oz.
    • Diana Ross co-stars as Dorothy. Jackson is said to wear his makeup long after production hours.
  • 1979
    • Jackson's fifth solo album, Off the Wall, established him as a solo performer and helped him move from the bubblegum pop of his youth to more complex sounds.
    • It produced four top 10 entries in the US: "Off the Wall", "She's Out of My Life", and the chart-topping singles "Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough" and "Rock with You"
    • The album reached number three on the US Billboard 200 and sold over 20 million copies worldwide.
  • 1980
    • Jackson won three American Music Awards for his solo work: Favorite Soul/R&B Album, Favorite Soul/R&B Male Artist, and Favorite Soul/R&B Single for "Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough".
    • He also won a Grammy Award for Best Male R&B Vocal Performance for 1979 with "Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough".
  • 1982-1983
    • Following the release of his 1979 hit album Off The Wall, Jackson released Thriller on Nov. 30, 1982.
    • Both albums were inducted into the GRAMMY Hall Of Fame in 2008, but Thriller is widely considered Jackson's most successful album
    • It was the first album to produce seven Billboard Hot 100 top-10 singles, including "Billie Jean", "Beat It", and "Wanna Be Startin' Somethin'"
  • 1984
    • Thriller won eight awards, and Jackson won an award for the E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial storybook.
    • Winning eight Grammys in one ceremony is a record he holds with the band Santana.
    • "Beat It" won Record of the Year and Best Rock Vocal Performance (Male).
    • "Billie Jean" won two Grammy awards: Best R&B Song and Best R&B Vocal Performance (Male), with Jackson as songwriter and singer respectively.
  • 1985
    • Jackson's changing appearance, and some wonder if the singer has had plastic surgery
    • The change drew widespread media coverage, including speculation that he had been bleaching his skin.
    • He builds a home on 2,700 acres in Central California, complete with its own amusement park rides, and calls it Neverland.
    • "We Are The World," a charity single for African hunger relief, is composed by Jackson and Lionel Richie.
    • Notable artists including Ray Charles, Stevie Wonder, and Cyndi Lauper contribute their voices.
    • This record-breaking single sells an astounding seven million copies.
  • 1987
    • Jackson's third studio album, Bad, is launched. This release initiates a global tour.
  • 1988
    • Jackson's autobiography, Moonwalk, is published by Doubleday.
  • 1990
    • Thriller achieves platinum status for the 21st time and is acknowledged as the best-selling album of all time by the Guinness Book of World Records, with a total of 65 million copies sold.
  • 1992
    • In an interview with Oprah Winfrey, Jackson shares his experience living with vitiligo, a skin condition that reduces melanin.
    • He also admits to suffering emotional abuse from his father during his childhood.
  • 1993
    • Jackson faces civil charges of sexually abusing a minor.
    • Following a full-body search by the police at his Neverland estate, Jackson publicly describes it as "the most humiliating ordeal of my life."
  • 1994
    • Jackson resolves the sexual abuse case outside court, resulting in a payout exceeding $15 million to the victim, held in trust until adulthood.
    • Both parents of the boy receive $1.5 million each. In the same year, Jackson marries Lisa-Marie Presley, but the union only lasts under two years.
  • 1995
    • Sony introduces HIStory: Past, Present and Future Book I. Jackson collaborates with his sister Janet on the duet, "Scream."
  • 1996
    • During the BRIT Awards, Jarvis Cocker of the UK band Pulp interrupts Jackson's "Earth Song" performance, alleging that Jackson was trying to impersonate Christ.
  • 1997
    • Jackson weds nurse Debbie Rowe. They have a son, Prince Michael, and Jackson is recognized in the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 1998
    • Rowe gives birth to their daughter, Paris Michael Katherine.
  • 1998
    • Rowe gives birth to their daughter, Paris Michael Katherine.
  • 1999
    • Jackson and Rowe separate.
  • 2000
    • Rolling Stone magazine recognizes "Billie Jean," "Rock With You," "I Want You Back," and "Beat It" in their 100 greatest songs of all time list.
  • 2001
    • Invincible is released by Sony, receiving poor reviews and underwhelming sales. Jackson also faces a lawsuit from a German concert promoter claiming the singer cancelled two concerts and kept an advance.
  • 2002
    • Jackson creates controversy by danging his newborn son, Prince Michael, over a hotel balcony.
    • The child's mother remains anonymous, and Jackson discloses that the child was conceived through artificial insemination.
  • 2003
    • Jackson faces criminal charges including seven counts of child sexual abuse and two counts of administering an intoxicating agent, all related to one boy, Gavin Arvizo.
  • 2005
    • Jackson is found not guilty on all charges in the Arvizo case.
  • 2006
    • Due to financial difficulties, the main house on the Neverland Ranch closes.
    • Jackson makes a public appearance in London to receive eight records from the Guinness World Records, including "Most Successful Entertainer of All Time".
  • 2007
    • Jackson and Sony acquire Famous Music LLC from Viacom, securing rights to songs by artists like Eminem, Shakira, and Beck.
  • 2008
    • Jackson celebrates 25 years of Thriller with Thriller 25, which ranks high in numerous countries.
    • Sony also releases King of Pop, a fan-voted compilation.
  • 2009
    • Jackson, age 50, tragically dies in Los Angeles from cardiac arrest.
